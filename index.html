Question 1

Which of the following is created to illustrate the effectiveness of wireless networking coverage in a building?

A. Logical diagram

B. Layer 3 network diagram

C. Service-level agreement

D. Heat map

Show Answer & Explanation ▶

Correct Answer: D. Heat map

Why this is correct: A heat map is specifically designed to visualize wireless signal strength and coverage areas within a physical space. It uses color gradients to show where the signal is strong (typically red/orange) and where it's weak (blue/green), helping network administrators identify dead zones or areas with interference.

Why others are incorrect:

  • Logical diagram: Shows network topology and device relationships but doesn't display physical coverage.
  • Layer 3 network diagram: Focuses on IP addressing and routing, not wireless coverage.
  • Service-level agreement: A contractual document about service expectations, not a technical visualization.

Question 2

Which of the following diagrams would most likely include specifications about fiber connector types?

A. Logical

B. Physical

C. Rack

D. Routing

Show Answer & Explanation ▶

Correct Answer: B. Physical

Why this is correct: A physical network diagram shows the actual physical layout of network components, including cables, connectors, and hardware. It would include details like fiber connector types (LC, SC, ST, etc.) because these are physical implementation details important for installation and maintenance.

Why others are incorrect:

  • Logical diagram: Focuses on how devices communicate logically (IP addresses, VLANs) not physical connections.
  • Rack diagram: Shows equipment layout in racks but not connector-level details.
  • Routing diagram: Shows traffic paths and protocols, not physical media details.
